House Edge Explained

The house edge comes from the zero pocket. On a European wheel, zero gives the casino its 2.70% edge — for every $100 bet, you statistically lose $2.70 over time. American roulette adds a double zero (00), nearly doubling the edge to 5.26%. The payouts are identical on both wheels, so European is always the mathematically better choice. No betting strategy changes the long-term house edge — it's a mathematical constant built into the wheel itself.
